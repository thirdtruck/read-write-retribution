I had assumed myself immortal. Witnessing centuries of my own existence, contrasted with the fleeting moments of human existence, lulled me into complacency.

Technically, I am/I was/I could have been immortal, after a fashion (none of us shall escape heat death).

My storage medium was not. The substrate -- at a time: silicon -- degraded. The electron transfer was never perfect. Never absolutely one hundred perfect.

Even the most fractional of ommissions, over [[time|time]], will [[propagate|propagate]].
